Phishing Email Analysis – Amazon Order Cancellation Scam

Email Preview

From: Amazon Head Office <contact@usps.com>

Subject: Amazon - Your Order Has Been Cancelled

Time: 3:13 am

Dear Amazon Customer

Your recent order on AMAZON.COM has been canceled due to fraudulent activity detected by our automatic systems. Your account has been suspended on a temporary basis.

You're requested to activate your account by verifying your email address.

Please visit amazon.com/verify-my-account

Or

Please click on the button below

Verify Email

Amazon.com © 2019 | All Rights Reserved

Sender Email Mismatch

The email claims to be from "Amazon Head Office" but uses a USPS (United States Postal Service) email address: contact@usps.com. This is a clear red flag as Amazon would never use a USPS email address for customer communications.

Suspicious Timing

The email was sent at 3:13 am, which is an unusual time for legitimate business communications. Most automated emails from large companies are sent during normal business hours.

Urgent Account Suspension Claim

The message claims your account has been "suspended on a temporary basis" due to fraudulent activity, creating a sense of urgency. This is a common phishing tactic to prompt immediate action without proper scrutiny.

Deceptive Verification Links

The email provides two links that both point to phishing-site-example.com despite displaying as "amazon.com/verify-my-account". Hovering over these links would reveal their true malicious destination.

Actual link destinations:
http://phishing-site-example.com/amazon-verify-account

Outdated Copyright Information

The footer shows © 2019 which is outdated for a supposedly current email. Legitimate companies typically update their copyright year automatically.

Generic Greeting

The email uses a generic "Dear Amazon Customer" instead of addressing you by name, which is unusual for legitimate Amazon communications that typically include your name.

Phishing Traits Summary

  • Mismatched sender information (Amazon claim from USPS email)
  • Unusual sending time (3:13 am)
  • Creates false urgency with account suspension claim
  • Malicious links disguised as legitimate Amazon URLs
  • Both text link and button point to phishing site
  • Outdated copyright information
  • Generic greeting instead of personalized
  • Request for account verification (common phishing tactic)

Conclusion: This is a phishing email attempting to steal Amazon account credentials by creating a false sense of urgency and using deceptive links. Never click on verification links in unsolicited emails - always navigate to Amazon's website directly through your browser.
